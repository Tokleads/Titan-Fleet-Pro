### Slide 3: The Problem (Identifying the Gap)
**Headline:** Friction in Safety-Critical Workflows
* **The Situation:** Drivers performing 15-minute "walk-around" checks in busy, low-light depots.
* **The Evidence:** Paper-based systems resulted in "ticking boxes" without actual inspection (Rubber-stamping).
* **The Risk:** 15% of fleet vehicles were operating with expired MOTs or safety defects due to manual record-keeping errors.
* **The Goal:** Shift from "compliance as a chore" to "compliance as a guided interaction."

### Slide 4: User Research (The Context)
**Headline:** Designing for the "Constrained User"
* **Methodology:** Direct observation and "shadowing" at the depot (6:00 AM starts).
* **Key Insight:** Drivers aren't "unskilled"—they are **distracted and physically constrained** (gloves, high-glare sunlight, oily hands).
* **Accessibility Finding:** Small touch targets on standard forms were the primary cause of data entry abandonment.
* **Outcome:** Research dictated a "Mobile-First, High-Contrast" design system.



### Slide 6: The Work (The Interface)
**Headline:** Evidence-Led Interaction Design
* **Key Feature:** Real-time DVSA MOT API integration.
* **Interaction Logic:** The app pre-populates vehicle data, reducing "Redundant Entry" (WCAG 2.2).
* **Visual Hierarchy:** * **Primary:** Safety-critical MOT status (Red/Green).
    * **Secondary:** Maintenance logs.
* **Senior Decision:** Removed all non-essential decorative elements to focus purely on the task at hand (minimizing cognitive load).

### Slide 7: Accessibility & Inclusivity
**Headline:** Beyond the Checklist: WCAG 2.2 AA in Practice
* **Target Size:** Increased all interactive elements to **44x44px** to account for "fat-finger" errors in cold weather.
* **Cognitive Load:** Implemented a "One-Thing-Per-Screen" flow to support neurodivergent users and those with low digital literacy.
* **Contrast:** Tested for 7:1 contrast ratios to ensure readability in direct sunlight (exceeding AA requirements).

### Slide 8: Prototyping Strategy
**Headline:** Proving Logic Through Functional Prototypes
* **Workflow:** Used an AI-native stack (Replit/React) to move from sketch to "Live" code in hours.
* **Why Code?** Static mocks can't test API latency or real-world keyboard/touch interactions.
* **Testing:** Ran "Guerilla testing" sessions in the depot using the functional prototype to gather high-fidelity feedback on the API response timing.

### Slide 10: Outcome & Impact
**Headline:** Delivering Measurable Value
* **Compliance:** Reached **100% digital record-keeping** within the first 30 days.
* **Efficiency:** Reduced the average time per check from 12 minutes (paper) to 7 minutes (digital) while increasing accuracy.
* **Scalability:** The design pattern is now being adapted for wider fleet management modules.
* **The "Senior" Takeaway:** Good design didn't just look better; it made the road safer.
