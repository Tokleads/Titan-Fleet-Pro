The idea is simple:

Instead of just selling software, you become the information hub for your industry.

When people search anything related to fleet compliance or operations, your site appears.

For TitanFleet, the moat would be built around UK transport compliance.

The TitanFleet Content Moat

Think of the site as three layers of content.

Top Layer: Guides and Education
Middle Layer: Tools and Comparisons
Bottom Layer: Product Pages

The top layer attracts traffic.
The bottom layer converts visitors into demos.

Layer 1 — Authority Guides

These are large “pillar” articles that cover major topics.

Examples:

The Complete DVSA Compliance Guide for Fleet Operators

The Ultimate Guide to HGV Walkaround Checks

Operator Licence Compliance Explained

Fleet Maintenance Best Practices for Transport Companies

How UK Haulage Companies Manage Compliance

These pages can attract thousands of visits over time.

Layer 2 — Supporting Articles

Each guide links to smaller topics.

Example under the DVSA Compliance Guide:

DVSA Walkaround Check Checklist

Driver Defect Reporting Explained

How Transport Managers Prepare for DVSA Audits

What Records Fleets Must Keep

Common DVSA Compliance Failures

These smaller articles support the main guide.

Layer 3 — Product Pages

Inside those articles you link to your solutions.

Example:

From article:

DVSA Walkaround Check Checklist

Internal links:

→ DVSA Walkaround Check App
→ Fleet Compliance Software
→ Driver Defect Reporting App

That pushes authority toward TitanFleet pages.
